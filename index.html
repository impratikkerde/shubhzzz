Hunger and Food Shortages Since 2002

In 2002, as Narayani was born, the world was already facing a silent crisis: millions of people struggling with hunger and food insecurity. Over the years, this challenge has only grown more urgent, with communities across continents experiencing shortages, rising food prices, and unequal access to nutrition.

Reports highlight that hunger is not just about empty stomachs — it is about lost opportunities, weakened health, and diminished futures. Since 2002, countless families have faced the harsh reality of not knowing where their next meal will come from.

Addressing hunger requires collective action: sustainable farming, fair distribution, and global solidarity. Narayani’s birth year reminds us that this issue has persisted for decades, and it is our responsibility to ensure that future generations inherit a world where food security is a right, not a privilege.
